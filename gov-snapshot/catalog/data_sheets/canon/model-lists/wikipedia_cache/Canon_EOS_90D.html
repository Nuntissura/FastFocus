The Canon EOS 90D is a digital single-lens reflex (DSLR) camera released by Canon on August 28, 2019.[4] It has a body-only MSRP of US$1199, which is the same as the Canon EOS 80D, which it replaces.[5]

The camera is one of Canon's APS-C cameras part of the Canon EOS two-digit line, it is the successor to the EOS 80D and is referred to as an enthusiast model.[6] The 90D has a 32.5 Megapixel APS-C image CMOS sensor.[7] The 90D is offered as a body only, with the EF-S 18-55mm IS STM kit lens or the EF-S 18-135mm IS USM kit lens.

New features the EOS 90D improved over the 80D

[edit]

The Canon EOS 90D introduces several key improvements over its predecessor, the 80D, including:

  • An aforementioned 32.5 megapixel APS-C CMOS sensor[1]
  • The faster DIGIC 8 processor[1]
  • 4K UHD video recording without a crop[8]
  • 45-point cross-type autofocus system[1]
  • Faster continuous shooting up to 10 frames per second[1]
    • Up 7 frames per second in live-view[1]
  • Dual Pixel CMOS AF with 5481 phase-detection points
  • Bluetooth[1]
  • A more durable weather-sealed body[9]
  • Improved battery life which offers a significant boost in performance and versatility for both photography and video[8]
  • A joystick which offers the ability to move the focus point more easily, which makes the 90D the first Canon EOS two-digit line body since the 50D to include one
